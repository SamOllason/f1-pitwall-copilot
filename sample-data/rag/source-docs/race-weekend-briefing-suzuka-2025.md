# Race Weekend Briefing - Suzuka 2025

## Context

- Season: 2025
- Event: Japanese Grand Prix
- Circuit: Suzuka
- Conditions outlook: mild air temp, moderate tailwind into Turn 1, low rain risk

## Track Characteristics

- High commitment changes of direction in Sector 1.
- Tire energy peaks through Esses and 130R approach.
- Overtake chances depend on exit quality from final chicane.

## Baseline Performance Readout

- Long-run average: +0.16s vs benchmark
- Qualifying simulation: strongest sector is Sector 3, weakest is Sector 1
- Tire behavior:
  - Medium: broad operating window
  - Soft: best one-lap, thermal fade if pushed over two push laps
  - Hard: consistent but lower peak pace

## Historical Pattern at Similar Tracks

- Time loss grows when front tire temp rises too fast in opening laps.
- Small setup errors in front response become larger through Esses rhythm.
- Driver confidence increases sharply once first clean sector is achieved.

## Setup Direction

- Front flap: -0.5 step from prior baseline to control peak front temp.
- Differential mid-corner: slight unlock for rotation through Esses.
- Engine braking: map tuned to smooth weight transfer into medium-speed entries.

## Strategy Pointers

- Preferred baseline: Medium -> Hard.
- Protect fronts in first 6 laps; avoid over-attack in dirty air.
- Safety car windows favor extending first stint if medium wear remains linear.
