# Race Weekend Briefing - Silverstone 2025

## Context

- Season: 2025
- Event: British Grand Prix
- Circuit: Silverstone
- Conditions outlook: cool mornings, gusting crosswinds at Maggotts-Becketts, medium chance of light rain on race day

## Key Objectives

1. Protect rear tire life in long high-speed sections.
2. Improve traction consistency out of Luffield and Club.
3. Avoid time loss from unstable braking into Brooklands.

## Baseline Performance Readout

- Long-run average (high fuel): +0.21s vs benchmark
- Qualifying simulation: +0.08s sector loss concentrated in Sector 2
- Degradation trend:
  - Hard: stable, slow warm-up
  - Medium: best overall race pace
  - Soft: strong launch pace, sharp drop after 7 laps

## Comparable Historical Weaknesses (cool, windy events)

- Repeated over-rotation at high-speed direction changes.
- Driver confidence drops after rear instability events.
- Extra steering correction increases front-left temperature.

## Driver Notes

### Lando

- Requests more rear support at high speed without killing rotation.
- Reports snap risk when committing early on turn-in.
- Comfortable extending medium stint if entry stability improves.

### Oscar

- Prefers sharper front response, accepts minor rear movement.
- Better recovery when rear slips in fast corners.

## Recommended Setup Direction

- Rear wing: +1 step from baseline.
- Differential entry: slightly more locked for stability.
- Brake migration: small rearward move late in braking phase.
- Front anti-roll bar: keep neutral to avoid front-left overload.

## Strategy Pointers

- Preferred race path: Medium -> Hard (one stop), with rain contingency.
- Undercut viability: moderate, mostly if traffic-free out-lap.
- If light rain appears in final third, delay switch unless track delta exceeds 1.8s.
