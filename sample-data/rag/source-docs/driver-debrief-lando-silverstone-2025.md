# Driver Debrief - Lando - Silverstone 2025

## Session Summary

- Driver: Lando Norris
- Event: British Grand Prix
- Circuit: Silverstone
- Focus: stability at high speed, traction on exit, repeatability over stint

## What Felt Good

- Car balance in low-speed corners was acceptable.
- Braking confidence improved once fuel came down.
- Medium tire had consistent grip window from lap 4 to lap 15.

## Recurring Issues

1. Rear instability in rapid direction changes (Maggotts-Becketts sequence).
2. Snap on throttle application when rotating at corner entry.
3. Traction inconsistency out of Luffield on worn mediums.
4. Minor confidence loss after one large rear movement event.

## Evidence Patterns

- Time loss spikes after instability events were usually seen over next 2-3 laps.
- Steering trace shows correction-heavy exits in Sector 2.
- Rear tire surface temperature rose quickly after correction events.

## Driver Verbatim Highlights

- "When I commit early at high speed, I cannot trust rear support."
- "I lose confidence for a couple of laps after one snap."
- "If rear is calmer on entry, I can carry speed and protect fronts."

## Engineering Interpretation

- Entry instability is reducing corner commitment.
- Reduced commitment shifts load profile and harms exit phase quality.
- Confidence effects are measurable in short windows after instability.

## Action Items (next similar circuit)

- Trial rear ride-height micro-adjustment for high-speed platform support.
- Keep medium-first race strategy unless ambient temperature rises sharply.
- Add simulator run focused on confidence rebuild after transient rear slides.
